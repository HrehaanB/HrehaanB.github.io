The Robotic Music Teacher

Learning that kids can hear, see, and feel.

BoomBot is an educational, percussive robot that plays Boomwhackers to introduce the creative intersection of music and robotics. Designed to be welcoming, durable, and kid‑friendly, BoomBot invites students to pick up an instrument, play along, and discover STEAM through rhythm.

Title I or STEM magnet, small groups or assemblies—BoomBot adapts to meet students where they are and turns curiosity into confidence.

What is BoomBot?

BoomBot is a hands‑on learning experience built around a percussive robot and entry‑level instruments called Boomwhackers. Students meet a friendly, expressive robot, learn rhythms and melodies, and peek under the hood at circuits, microcontrollers, and code. It’s simple on purpose—so kids can touch, play, and learn without fear.

Watch a quick demo of BoomBot in action.

Three pillars. One mission.

Every session blends Music (confidence & expression), Robotics (curiosity & problem‑solving), and Community (access & inclusion). The goal: make STEAM feel human, joyful, and fun for everyone.

Music

From “Hot Cross Buns” to classroom favorites, students learn rhythm, patterns, and melody by performing with the robot. Music becomes a doorway to pattern recognition, timing, and teamwork.

Robotics

Behind the beats: microcontrollers, sensors, and motors. Students see how inputs become motion and sound—then try it themselves, safely and simply.

Community

Partnering with elementary schools, local libraries, and organizations like SEWA, BoomBot brings STEAM experiences to Title I schools and diverse communities—because accessibility matters.

From PVC to friend: the BoomBot journey

A simple question started it: What if a robot could teach rhythm? The journey since has been equal parts persistence, prototyping, and play.

01

Sketches & snack‑table prototypes

Cardboard arms, rubber mallets, and a lot of trial‑and‑error. The goal: safely strike Boomwhackers with consistent tone.

02

Electronics & expressiveness

Microcontrollers, motor control, and LED faces to mirror emotions—angry, sad, silly—so kids connect with the robot.

03

Classroom pilots

Small‑group sessions validate the flow: learn a beat, peek at the circuits, perform together. Feedback drives tweaks for durability and timing.

04

Scaling access

Partnerships with local schools and service orgs expand reach. Kit‑style materials and lesson plans make visits easy and repeatable.

What a session looks like

  1. Meet BoomBot & the Boomwhackers (friendly faces + safety check).
  2. Play along: rhythm‑call and response, then a short song.
  3. Peek inside: see how code becomes sound and motion.
  4. Create: students try a pattern, then perform together.

Ideal group size: ~7 students per mini‑block for a personal, hands‑on experience.
